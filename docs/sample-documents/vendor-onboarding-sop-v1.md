## 1. Purpose

This procedure defines the minimum steps required to request, assess, approve, activate, and periodically review vendors used by Northstar Cloud. Its purpose is to ensure that vendor relationships have clear business ownership, proportionate risk review, documented approvals, and traceable evidence before a vendor can provide services, access systems, or receive payment.

## 2. Scope

This procedure applies to new vendors that will provide goods or services, access Northstar Cloud systems, process Northstar Cloud or customer information, or receive payment from Northstar Cloud. It does not replace contract negotiation, incident-response, or payment-execution procedures.

## 3. Roles and responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| --- | --- |
| Business Requester | Documents the business need, expected spend, requested service, and internal owner. |
| Procurement Operations | Coordinates intake, confirms completeness, classifies vendor risk, and maintains the onboarding record. |
| Finance / Accounts Payable | Independently validates payment-related information and creates the vendor record after approval. |
| Security and Privacy | Reviews vendors that meet security or data-access criteria. |
| Legal | Reviews non-standard contractual terms when requested by Procurement or the Business Requester. |
| Compliance | Reviews policy, regulatory, or screening requirements when the risk tier requires it. |
| Final Approver | Accepts high-risk vendors and approved exceptions. |

## 4. Procedure

### 4.1 Vendor request intake

The Business Requester must submit a request before engaging a new vendor. Each request must include the vendor legal name, requested service, business owner, expected annual spend, target start date, service location, expected system access, and the types of Northstar Cloud or customer information the vendor may process.

Procurement Operations must check whether an approved or pending vendor record already exists before starting a new request. Potential duplicates must be resolved before the request proceeds.

### 4.2 Risk classification

Procurement Operations must classify each vendor as standard, enhanced, or high risk before activation.

| Risk tier | Criteria | Minimum review path |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Standard | Expected annual spend is below $10,000, no access to Northstar Cloud systems, and no processing of customer information. | Procurement Operations review and standard approval. |
| Enhanced | Expected annual spend is $10,000 or more, or the vendor supports an important internal business process. | Procurement Operations and Finance review; additional review when triggered by the service. |
| High | Expected annual spend is $25,000 or more; the vendor processes customer personal data; the vendor accesses production systems or customer environments; or the vendor provides a business-critical service. | Procurement, Finance, Security and Privacy, Compliance, and Final Approver review. |

If more than one criterion applies, Procurement Operations must use the highest applicable risk tier.

### 4.3 Required information and evidence

All vendor requests must include the vendor legal name, registered address, primary business contact, service description, requested start date, and documented business owner.

Enhanced and high-risk vendors must also provide applicable evidence, including relevant insurance certificates, required licenses or certifications, and information needed to complete the assigned review path.

Payment information must be collected only through the approved Finance process. The Business Requester and Procurement Operations must not approve or activate a vendor's payment details.

### 4.4 Required reviews

Security and Privacy must review a vendor before approval when the vendor will process customer personal data, access production systems, connect to Northstar Cloud through an integration, or receive credentials for a Northstar Cloud system.

Finance / Accounts Payable must independently verify payment details using an approved method that is separate from the vendor-submitted request. Finance must record the verification outcome before a vendor record can be activated.

Legal review is required for non-standard contractual terms, a requested limitation of liability, or a vendor that will process customer personal data under a non-standard data-processing arrangement.

Compliance review is required when the high-risk review path is selected or when Procurement identifies a policy, regulatory, sanctions, or licensing concern.

### 4.5 Approval requirements

A standard vendor requires Procurement Operations approval.

An enhanced vendor requires Procurement Operations and Finance approval.

A high-risk vendor requires Procurement Operations, Finance, Security and Privacy, Compliance, and Final Approver approval. Legal approval is also required when the legal-review criteria in section 4.4 apply.

No person may request, approve, create, and activate the same vendor record alone.

### 4.6 Exceptions

An exception to this procedure requires a written business justification, description of the residual risk, compensating controls, and an expiration date no more than 30 calendar days after approval.

The Final Approver and the owner of each omitted review must approve the exception before the vendor is activated. Procurement Operations must record the exception and notify the affected reviewers.

### 4.7 Vendor activation

Finance / Accounts Payable may create and activate the vendor record only after all required approvals, evidence, and payment-detail verification are complete.

Procurement Operations must update the onboarding record with the final risk tier, approval status, and links to supporting evidence. A vendor that is missing required approvals must remain pending and cannot receive a purchase order or payment.

### 4.8 Periodic review

Procurement Operations must review active high-risk vendors at least annually. The review must confirm the vendor's risk tier, active service, required evidence, and open exceptions.

A material change to a vendor's service, ownership, data access, system access, payment details, or risk tier requires reassessment before the change takes effect.

### 4.9 Records and retention

Procurement Operations must retain the request, risk assessment, review outcomes, approvals, exceptions, and supporting evidence for seven years after vendor deactivation, unless a longer retention period is required by applicable policy or law.
